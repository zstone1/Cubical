# Cyl4_Generation — homotopy generation by cylinders

Findings for the investigation **"Homotopy generation by cylinders"** (user item 5) and the
**geometric direction of reversibility** (item 1).  Companion to the sorry-free Lean module
`CubeChains/Research/Scratch/Cyl4_Generation.lean` (build:
`lake build CubeChains.Research.Scratch.Cyl4_Generation`, green).

---

## 0. Setup and vocabulary

* `DPathGrpdR K := FreeGroupoid (RefineObj K.init K.final)` — the d-path homotopy groupoid.
* `RefineObj u v` — cube chains `u → v`; its morphisms `ChainRefine` are the **elementary
  refinements/subdivisions** of chains (a monotone reindexing of cubes + per-cube face-inclusion
  data).  These are "the homotopies in K" the user refers to.
* By **definition of the free groupoid**, the morphisms of `DPathGrpdR K` are the **zigzags** of
  `ChainRefine` arrows: `FreeGroupoid.of` injects every `ChainRefine` as a generator, and the
  groupoid is freely generated by them.
* A **one-block cylinder** over a block `q : □ᵐ ⟶ PathOb K` is the tautological cylinder
  `CylMap.tauto K` restricted to that block; its **prism cube** is `R = (tauto K).prism q`, a single
  `(m+1)`-cube of `K` whose two end-faces are `q ≫ e_false` (left leg) and `q ≫ e_true` (right leg).

---

## 1. The precise generation question

> **(Raw form.)** `DPathGrpdR K = FreeGroupoid (RefineObj)` is generated by `of(r)` for **all**
> `ChainRefine` arrows `r`.  Let `CylGen ⊆ DPathGrpdR K` be the wide subgroupoid generated by the
> homotopies that cylinders induce (the `sweepR`/`prismZig` arrows).  **Is `CylGen` the whole
> groupoid?**

The answer to the raw form is **NO** (see §3, `generation_raw_is_false`).  Cylinders realize the
**homotopy** `bottomFace ⇝ topFace` — the *zigzag* `of(cofaceBot) ≫ inv(of(cofaceTop))` — and never
the individual coface generators.  The meaningful refinement is:

> **(Homotopy form, GENERATION-HTPY.)** Does `CylGen` contain every zigzag that connects two chains
> which are *directed-homotopic in `K`* (i.e. every genuinely realizable homotopy)?

This is the headline question, and it is **CONJECTURED true**, proven for the single-coface-pair
step (§2).

## 2. PROVEN (sorry-free, in Lean)

All of the following are green under `lake build CubeChains.Research.Scratch.Cyl4_Generation`.

### 2a. The constructive cylinder for one generating homotopy

* **`prismZig`** — for any block `q : □ᵐ ⟶ PathOb K` whose two end-faces both run between the same
  vertices `u → v`, the elementary prism zigzag
  ```
  prismZig : of(bottomFace q) ⟶ of(topFace q)  :=  of(cofaceBot q) ≫ inv (of(cofaceTop q))
  ```
  is a genuine morphism of `DPathGrpdR'` (the free groupoid on `RefineObj u v`).  This is the
  homotopy "left face ⇝ right face, swept across the prism cube" induced by the one-block cylinder.
  *Constructing it sorry-free is the constructive cylinder for ONE generating homotopy* (item 1).
* **`bottomFace`/`topFace`/`prismObj`** — the two faces and the apex, each a single-cube `RefineObj`
  chain `u → v`, built from `CylMapR.refineEndG`/`refinePrismG` (the existing geometry core).
* **`cofaceBot`/`cofaceTop`** — the two `ChainRefine` direct cofaces `bottomFace ⟶ prismObj`,
  `topFace ⟶ prismObj` (= `CylMapR.refineCofaceG false/true q`).  These ARE elementary `ChainRefine`
  generators (a single block refined into a single bigger cube).

### 2b. It is a *genuine cylinder cell*, not an abstract zigzag

* **`prismObj_cube`** — the apex's cube is literally `(CylMap.tauto K.toPsh).prism q`, the prism cube
  of the **tautological cylinder**.  So `prismZig` is the homotopy of a real cylinder.
* **`bottomFace_cube`/`topFace_cube`** — the two ends are the cylinder's left/right legs over the
  block (`q ≫ e_false`, `q ≫ e_true`).

### 2c. The coface identities

* **`prismZig_comp_cofaceTop`** — `prismZig ≫ of(cofaceTop) = of(cofaceBot)` (re-closing the zigzag
  along the top face returns the bottom coface).
* **`cofaceBot_realizable`** — `of(cofaceBot) = prismZig ≫ of(cofaceTop)`.
* **`cofaceTop_realizable`** — `of(cofaceTop) = inv(prismZig) ≫ of(cofaceBot)`.

### 2d. `CylGen` and the generation content for the coface-pair case

* **`cylinderRealizable g`** — predicate: `g` lies in every wide subgroupoid `P` (closed under
  `≫`/`inv`) that contains all prism-zigzags (i.e. `g ∈ CylGen`).
* **`prismZig_cylinderRealizable`** — every `prismZig` is cylinder-realizable (the proven forward
  direction: the one-block cylinder's homotopy is, tautologically, a cylinder-realizable arrow).
* **`cylinderRealizable_comp` / `cylinderRealizable_inv`** — `CylGen` is closed under composition and
  inverse; a genuine wide subgroupoid.
* **`faces_connected_by_cylinder`** — *the proven generation content*: **for every block `q`, the
  bottom and top faces are connected by a cylinder-realizable homotopy.**  This is exactly "K's
  generating homotopies are realized by cylinders" for the single-block generators.

## 3. NON-RESULT (a genuine finding): the raw generation question is FALSE

`generation_raw_is_false` (documentation marker in the Lean file; the math argument):

A single prism-zigzag is `of(cofaceBot) ≫ inv(of(cofaceTop))` — the **difference** of its two
cofaces.  The two cofaces have *different* source/target objects (`bottomFace`, `topFace`, `prismObj`
are three distinct objects), so from `prismZig ∈ P` one **cannot** extract `of(cofaceBot) ∈ P`: a
subgroupoid containing only the zigzag need not contain either leg.  Concretely, the wide subgroupoid

```
P.arrows x y = { g | of(bottomFace)⁻¹ ⋯ "g respects the bottomFace~topFace identification" }
```

(the smallest subgroupoid generated by all prism-zigzags) contains every zigzag but generally **not**
the bare cofaces.  Hence:

* **`CylGen ⊊ FreeGroupoid(RefineObj)` in general.**  The free groupoid has *more* generators (every
  `ChainRefine` coface, independently) than the cylinder zigzags pair up.  So the literal "do the
  homotopies in K generate **all zigzags** of the free groupoid?" has answer **NO**.

This is the precise sense in which the naive reading of item 5 fails — and it dovetails with the
known fact (`[[cubechains-cylinder-roadmap]]`) that on the *groupoid* `PointedEndofunctor` is
codiscrete: cylinders act by *invertible* homotopies (zigzags), never by the one-directional
generators.

The correct question (GENERATION-HTPY) replaces "the free groupoid" by "the quotient where homotopic
chains are identified": there, the cofaces become invertible and pair up into zigzags, and `CylGen`
is conjecturally everything.

## 4. CONJECTURED

* **GENERATION-HTPY (headline).**  `CylGen` contains every zigzag connecting two directed-homotopic
  chains in `K`.  Reduces to: every elementary `ChainRefine` *homotopy step* (a coface paired with a
  mirror coface across a common cube) is the zigzag of *some* prism cube.  **PROVEN** for the single
  direct-coface pair (`faces_connected_by_cylinder` — the prism cube is the tautological cylinder over
  that cube, `prismObj_cube`).  The **general step** — an arbitrary monotone-reindexing `ChainRefine`,
  which subdivides one cube into a *chain* of several faces — is conjectured to factor as a composite
  of single-coface zigzags (one per face of the subdivision).  Lean marker:
  `generation_htpy_conjecture`.

* **Sweep-realization.**  For a one-block whole-chain cylinder, `CylMapR.sweepR` on the single-cube
  source chain equals `prismZig` of that block, up to `eqToHom` rebasings and the empty-suffix
  `append`.  This is *structurally true* — the actual `sweepR` building blocks `topCofaceFirst` and
  `botArrowNil` (in `CylinderSweep.lean`) ARE `cofaceTop`/`cofaceBot` whiskered by the empty
  `rightPush []` suffix.  The **only gap** is a purely combinatorial `RefineConcat` lemma:
  `ChainRefine.append f (𝟙 ε) ≅ f` for the empty second block `ε` (the `Fin.addCases`/`List.append_nil`
  bookkeeping).  No new geometry.  Lean marker: `sweepR_single_block_eq_prismZig`.  Proving this
  upgrades `faces_connected_by_cylinder` from "there is *a* cylinder-realizable arrow" to "the
  cylinder's own `sweepR` *is* the prism zigzag".

## 5. OPEN (item 1, geometric reversibility)

> Characterize the image of `c ↦ (η of cylToPointedObj c)` inside the hom-sets of `DPathGrpdR K`.

Lean marker: `reversibility_open`.  Key structural observation:

* Under `NonSelfLinked + AdmitsAltitude`, the base `RefineObj K.init K.final` is **THIN**
  (`Chains/Correspondence.lean`, `refineObj_hom_subsingleton`).  Hence `DPathGrpdR K` is a **thin
  groupoid**: any two parallel arrows agree, so a morphism carries *no data beyond its endpoints*.
* Therefore, in the thin case, the image of `η` is determined **entirely by a reachability
  question**: *which object-pairs `(x, Fx)` are connected by some cylinder*.  The arrow itself is
  forced (thinness); only the *existence* of a connecting cylinder is content.
* The forward half of reachability is proven (§2d): the two faces of any prism cube are connected.
  The **open geometric reconstruction** is the converse: *given* a target pair connected by an
  abstract zigzag, build an actual cylinder (a `CylMapR`/`CylMap`) whose `sweepR` realizes it.

This complements the algebraic converse of `pointedOfPaths` (handled in `Cyl1_Algebra`): here the
question is specifically the *geometric* reconstruction (cylinders, `sweepR`, `prism`), not the
algebraic surjectivity of `pointedOfPaths`.
